About Me

I am a graduate researcher at USTB, working at the intersection of safe reinforcement learning, control barrier functions, and legged robotics. My research builds mathematical safety guarantees directly into policy design — through CBF-based probabilistic risk reformulation, physics-grounded reward shaping, and geometry-aware action parameterization — and validates them on both legged robot platforms and vehicle dynamics simulators. I am self-taught in RL and robotics with a formal background in vehicle engineering, and am actively applying for PhD positions (2027 Fall) at universities in Hong Kong. I am happy to connect with researchers sharing similar interests.

Publications

R²CBF
Automatica 2026
Response-Aware Risk-Constrained Control Barrier Function With Application to Vehicles
Qijun Liao*, Jue Yang  * Corresponding Author
Automatica, 2026
Under Review
We propose R²CBF, a distributionally robust Control Barrier Function framework that combines hybrid response-aware uncertainty modeling with CVaR tail-risk reformulation. Instead of conservative L∞ worst-case bounds, we enforce probabilistic safety over the tail distribution of the CBF derivative, driven by real-time body signals. Bayesian online covariance adaptation (Inverse Wishart conjugate prior) handles dynamic friction changes without real-time identification. Zero safety boundary violations across all extreme combined-condition scenarios, with per-step violation probability provably below risk level β.
H-EARS
Neurocomputing 2026
Hybrid Energy-Aware Reward Shaping: A Unified Lightweight Physics-Guided Methodology for Policy Optimization
Qijun Liao, Jue Yang*, Yiting Kang, Xinxin Zhao, Yong Zhang, Mingan Zhao
Neurocomputing, 2026
Reviews Completed · Paper
H-EARS introduces a dual-layer physics-guided reward shaping framework that provably separates task and energy objectives via functional independence — avoiding gradient conflicts common in unified potential designs. We prove that energy-based potentials provide informationally richer guidance than sparse task rewards, with convergence acceleration proportional to energy gradient magnitude. The O(n) modeling complexity (vs. full analytical mechanics) makes the method directly extensible to legged systems. Validated across MuJoCo benchmarks (Ant-v5, Hopper-v5, Humanoid-v5, LunarLander-v3) and TruckSim vehicle simulation.
VSD
Veh. Syst. Dyn. 2026
Wheel Dynamic Load Estimation Method Based on Gas Pressure of Hydro-pneumatic Suspension
Qijun Liao, Jue Yang*, Subhash Rakheja, Yiting Kang, Yumeng Yao, Yuming Yin
Vehicle System Dynamics, 2026
Under Review · Paper
We propose a wheel dynamic load estimation method that requires only a single gas pressure sensor, eliminating the need for force transducers. A nonlinear dynamics model explicitly accounting for fluid inertia in gas-oil coupling is established, with thermodynamic state equations integrating polytropic gas processes and flow-dependent damping. The core theoretical contribution is the Pressure-Velocity Uniqueness Mapping Theorem, which proves bijectivity and differentiability of the mapping p → v → F_z under fluid-inertia-dominated dynamics — guaranteeing unique load reconstruction from pressure trajectories. Validated with bench tests (force RMSE <4.2%) and TruckSim co-simulation on ISO-8608 Class C/D road profiles (wheel load RMSE <5%).
