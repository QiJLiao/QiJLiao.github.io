About

I am a graduate researcher at USTB, working at the intersection of safe reinforcement learning, control barrier functions, and legged robotics. My work builds mathematical safety guarantees directly into policy design — through CBF-based risk reformulation, physics-grounded reward shaping, and geometry-aware action parameterization — and validates them on both legged robot platforms and vehicle dynamics simulators. I am self-taught in RL and robotics with a formal background in vehicle engineering, and am actively applying for PhD positions (2027 Fall) at universities in Hong Kong.

Research

H-EARS
Hybrid Energy-Aware Reward Shaping
MAY 2025 – OCT 2025 · Neurocomputing (Reviews Completed)
Physics-guided dual-layer reward shaping. Proved functional independence of task and energy objectives, enabling gradient-conflict-free policy optimization with O(n) modeling cost. Validated on MuJoCo locomotion benchmarks (Ant, Hopper, Humanoid) and TruckSim vehicle simulation.
Physics-Guided RL Reward Shaping MuJoCo TruckSim
R²CBF
Response-Aware Risk-Constrained CBF
SEP 2025 – JAN 2026 · Automatica (Under Review)
Distributionally robust CBF with Bayesian online covariance adaptation (Inverse Wishart). CVaR tail-risk reformulation replaces conservative L∞ worst-case bounds. Zero safety violations across all extreme combined-condition scenarios vs. divergence in deterministic CBF baselines.
CBF CVaR Bayesian Online Learning SOCP
DD-SRad
Decoupled Dynamic Spherical Radial Squashing
OCT 2025 – PRESENT · In Preparation
Plug-and-play RL module enforcing per-dimension actuator slew-rate limits with probability 1, eliminating dimensional coupling pathologies in global-radius methods. Validated on Unitree A1 in Isaac Lab under physically realistic heterogeneous joint actuator limits.
Action Constraints Legged Robotics Isaac Lab SAC / TD3
Suspension Est.
Wheel Load Estimation via Gas Pressure
JAN 2025 – MAY 2025 · Vehicle System Dynamics (Under Review)
Single gas pressure sensor approach for wheel dynamic load reconstruction. Proved Pressure-Velocity Uniqueness Mapping Theorem under fluid-inertia dynamics. Bench-test force RMSE <4.2%; TruckSim co-simulation RMSE <5% across ISO-8608 Class C/D road profiles.
State Estimation Vehicle Dynamics TruckSim
